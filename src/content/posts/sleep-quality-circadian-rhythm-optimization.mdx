## The Biology of Circadian Rhythms

Nearly every cell in your body operates on a 24-hour clock controlled by genes that oscillate in expression. This internal timing system — the circadian rhythm — governs hormone release, body temperature fluctuation, immune cell mobilization, and cognitive performance throughout the day.

The master clock resides in the suprachiasmatic nucleus (SCN) of the hypothalamus and synchronizes via light exposure to the eyes. When your behavioral patterns (sleep, eating, light exposure) consistently misalign with this master clock, the result is called circadian disruption — a state strongly associated with metabolic disease, depression, and accelerated aging.

## The Four Stages of Sleep

**Stage 1 & 2 (Light Sleep)**
Transition from wakefulness. Heart rate and temperature drop.

**Stage 3 (Slow-Wave Sleep / Deep Sleep)**
The physically restorative phase. Human growth hormone peaks. Metabolic waste is cleared from the brain via the glymphatic system. Memory consolidation begins.

**REM (Rapid Eye Movement)**
The psychologically restorative phase. Emotional processing occurs. Creative problem-solving is integrated. Acetylcholine surges while norepinephrine shuts off — the only time the brain is flooded with creativity-linked neurochemicals without the anxiety-generating stress response.

Healthy adults cycle through these four stages 4–6 times per night in approximately 90-minute intervals.

## What Destroys Sleep Architecture

**Alcohol**
While it accelerates sleep onset, alcohol suppresses REM sleep and fragments sleep continuity. The result is that you spend more time unconscious but experience dramatically less restorative benefit.

**Late Eating**
Eating within 2–3 hours of bedtime raises core body temperature and keeps insulin elevated, both of which prevent the physiological cascade that initiates deep sleep.

**Irregular Sleep Timing**
Going to bed at highly variable times each night desynchronizes your circadian clock. This creates "social jet lag" — the metabolic equivalent of crossing time zones twice per week.

## Evidence-Based Sleep Optimization

**Morning Sunlight Exposure**
10–30 minutes of unfiltered morning light exposure synchronizes your circadian clock and advances melatonin secretion onset at night. This single habit produces more consistent bedtime drowsiness than any supplement.

**Temperature Regulation**
Your body needs to drop 2–3°F to initiate and maintain deep sleep. Keep your bedroom 65–68°F and consider a hot bath 90 minutes before bed — the post-bath cooling triggers sleep onset.

**Magnesium Glycinate**
200–400 mg before bed enhances parasympathetic nervous system tone and reduces cortisol.
