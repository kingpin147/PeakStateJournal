## The Convergence Point: What All Longevity Diets Share

Despite heated debates between advocates of ketogenic, Mediterranean, plant-based, and carnivore diets, population research reveals a striking convergence. The communities with the longest healthspans — Sardinians, Okinawans, Seventh-day Adventists in Loma Linda — all share four dietary patterns regardless of their specific foods:

1. Predominantly whole, minimally processed foods
2. High diversity of plant-based inputs
3. Moderate total caloric intake
4. Very low consumption of added sugars and refined grains

## The Mediterranean Diet: Best-Evidenced of All Patterns

The PREDIMED trial — the largest randomized controlled trial of dietary intervention for cardiovascular outcomes — demonstrated that a Mediterranean-style diet supplemented with extra virgin olive oil reduced major cardiovascular events by 30% compared to a low-fat diet.

### Core Components
- **Extra virgin olive oil** as primary fat source (4+ tablespoons daily in the trial)
- **Legumes** three or more times per week
- **Oily fish** at least twice per week
- **Nuts** daily (walnuts, almonds, hazelnuts)
- **Vegetables** with every meal
- **Minimal red meat** and very limited processed foods

## The Role of Polyphenols

Polyphenols — plant compounds in berries, dark chocolate, olive oil, and tea — activate cellular longevity pathways, including SIRT1, and reduce oxidative stress. The diversity of polyphenol intake appears more important than the quantity of any single source.

Aim for 30 different plant foods per week. This diversity measure correlates more strongly with gut microbiome health than any probiotic supplement.

## Practical Eating Framework

**At every meal, ask:** Is the majority of my plate from whole, recognizable food sources?

**Daily non-negotiables:**
- A large handful of leafy greens
- A source of omega-3 fatty acids
- Legumes or nuts
- A variety of colorful vegetables

**Weekly targets:**
- Fatty fish twice
- Organ meat or liver once (densest nutrient profile of any food)
- Fermented foods daily for gut diversity
