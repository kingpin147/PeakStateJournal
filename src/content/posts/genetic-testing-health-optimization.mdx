## The Genetic Revolution in Personal Health

Consumer genomics companies now offer access to your raw SNP (single nucleotide polymorphism) data — the specific genetic variants at millions of positions across your genome — for under $100. Third-party interpretation services can then analyze this data for health-relevant variants.

Understanding what this information can and cannot tell you is essential before acting on it.

## Where Genetic Testing Provides Genuine Actionable Value

**APOE4 Status (Alzheimer's Risk)**
The APOE4 allele is the most significant genetic risk factor for late-onset Alzheimer's disease. Carrying one copy roughly doubles risk; two copies increase it 8–12 fold. Knowing your APOE4 status enables dietary modifications (reduce saturated fat, optimize omega-3s), earlier cognitive monitoring, and motivational support for lifestyle optimization.

Important: APOE4 is not destiny. APOE4 carriers who maintain excellent metabolic health, exercise regularly, and avoid smoking show much lower actual disease rates than epidemiological averages suggest.

**MTHFR Variants (Methylation and B-Vitamin Processing)**
As covered separately, MTHFR C677T and A1298C variants impair folic acid processing, requiring methylated B-vitamin supplementation. This is among the most directly actionable genetic findings.

**Pharmacogenomics Variants (Drug Metabolism)**
CYP450 enzyme variants affect metabolism of dozens of common medications. Knowing your CYP2C19, CYP2D6, and CYP3A4 status helps physicians select appropriate medications and doses, avoiding adverse effects.

**Lactase Persistence / Lactose Tolerance**
The LCT gene variant explains individual variation in dairy tolerance — actionable dietary information.

## Where Genetic Testing Provides Misleading Signals

**Polygenic traits (height, IQ, complex disease risk)**
Most meaningful health outcomes are influenced by hundreds or thousands of variants, each contributing tiny individual effects. Polygenic risk scores from consumer tests have limited predictive power at the individual level and should not drive major health decisions.

**Ancestry-based disease claims**
Disease risk is population-average data, not individual destiny. Genes interact with environment in complex non-linear ways that no current test can fully model.

## How to Use Your Genetic Data Wisely

1. Run raw data through established clinical interpretation tools (Promethease, LiveWello, or physician-ordered clinical reports)
2. Focus on variants with established, actionable interventions
3. Validate genetic findings with corresponding blood tests
4. Work with a physician or genetic counselor before making major medical decisions
