## Why Fatigue Is Biochemically Complex

Fatigue is the most common presenting complaint in primary care — yet it remains chronically under-investigated. Most clinical evaluations stop at thyroid function and a basic complete blood count, missing the specific micronutrient deficiencies that are among the most tractable and reversible causes of persistent low energy.

## The Core Deficiency Panel

**1. Ferritin (Iron Storage)**
The standard hemoglobin test catches overt anemia but misses iron depletion — when ferritin (iron storage) is low while hemoglobin remains normal. This stage produces profound fatigue, reduced exercise tolerance, poor cognitive function, and impaired thyroid hormone conversion.

Optimal ferritin for energy: 70–100 ng/mL. Levels below 30 ng/mL consistently correlate with fatigue even in the absence of anemia.

**2. Vitamin B12 (Methylcobalamin)**
B12 deficiency impairs myelin synthesis, red blood cell formation, and neurological function. Symptoms include fatigue, brain fog, tingling extremities, and mood disturbances.

High-risk groups: vegans and vegetarians, anyone over 60 (reduced stomach acid impairs absorption), long-term metformin users, and those with autoimmune gastritis.

Optimal serum B12: above 400 pg/mL. Standard reference ranges typically mark anything above 200 pg/mL as "normal" — inadequate for neurological optimization.

**3. Thyroid (Full Panel: TSH, Free T3, Free T4, Reverse T3)**
Standard thyroid screening measures only TSH. This misses conversion dysfunction, where T4 is produced normally but fails to convert to the active T3 form — common in chronic stress and nutrient deficiency.

**4. Vitamin D**
Fatigue is among the most commonly reported symptoms of vitamin D deficiency. Target serum level: 40–60 ng/mL.

**5. Magnesium (RBC Magnesium)**
Standard serum magnesium reflects only 1% of total body stores and rarely shows deficiency until depletion is severe. Red blood cell magnesium testing provides a more accurate picture of cellular availability.

Magnesium is required for ATP synthesis — deficiency directly impairs cellular energy production at its most fundamental level.

## The Testing Protocol

Request a morning fasting blood draw including: ferritin, full iron panel, B12, folate, complete metabolic panel, thyroid panel (TSH + free T3 + free T4), 25-OH vitamin D, RBC magnesium, and zinc.

This comprehensive panel costs less than two months of random supplement purchases and provides actionable data.
