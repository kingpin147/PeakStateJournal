## Reading Your Thyroid Results: The Basic Framework

Thyroid function operates through a feedback loop between the pituitary gland and the thyroid gland. Understanding this feedback relationship is the key to interpreting your results logically rather than simply accepting a "normal" or "abnormal" label.

The pituitary gland monitors circulating thyroid hormone levels. When levels are too low, the pituitary secretes more TSH (thyroid-stimulating hormone) to tell the thyroid to produce more hormone. When levels are too high, TSH drops to suppress thyroid activity.

This means **TSH moves in the opposite direction from thyroid hormone levels:**
- High TSH = thyroid is being pushed hard = likely underactive (hypothyroid)
- Low TSH = thyroid doesn't need stimulation = likely overactive (hyperthyroid) or suppressed

## Common Result Patterns

### Pattern 1: High TSH + Low Free T4 = Overt Hypothyroidism
The thyroid is failing to produce adequate hormone. The pituitary is working overtime. This is the classic pattern that requires thyroid hormone replacement therapy.

**Symptoms:** Fatigue, weight gain, cold intolerance, constipation, dry skin, hair loss, depression, cognitive slowing, high cholesterol.

### Pattern 2: High TSH + Normal Free T4 = Subclinical Hypothyroidism
The pituitary is working harder than normal to maintain thyroid hormone in the normal range. This represents early thyroid dysfunction.

**Clinical significance:** Symptomatic patients with subclinical hypothyroidism often benefit from treatment. Asymptomatic patients with TSH below 10 mIU/L are typically monitored. TPO antibodies should be checked — their presence indicates Hashimoto's and predicts progression.

### Pattern 3: Normal TSH + Low Free T3 = Conversion Dysfunction
TSH is normal because T4 is being produced adequately. But conversion of T4 to active T3 is impaired. Standard screening (TSH only) completely misses this pattern.

**Common causes:** Chronic stress, selenium deficiency, systemic inflammation, low-calorie dieting, and certain medications.

**Symptoms:** Identical to hypothyroidism — fatigue, cold intolerance, weight resistance — despite "normal" thyroid tests.

### Pattern 4: Normal TSH + Normal Free T4 + Low Free T3 + High Reverse T3 = rT3 Dominance
All standard markers appear normal. Only by measuring reverse T3 and the Free T3:Reverse T3 ratio is the dysfunction revealed. This pattern is common in anyone under chronic physical or psychological stress.

### Pattern 5: Low TSH + High Free T4 + High Free T3 = Overt Hyperthyroidism
The thyroid is overproducing hormone. Common causes include Graves' disease (autoimmune TSH receptor stimulation) and toxic nodular goiter.

**Symptoms:** Weight loss despite good appetite, palpitations, heat intolerance, anxiety, tremor, increased bowel movements, insomnia.

### Pattern 6: Normal TSH + Elevated TPO Antibodies = Hashimoto's Without Dysfunction Yet
Autoimmune attack on the thyroid is occurring, but compensatory mechanisms are maintaining normal hormone levels. This pattern predicts future hypothyroidism and warrants monitoring every 6–12 months.

## When to Request a Full Panel

Request TSH + Free T4 + Free T3 + Reverse T3 + TPO antibodies if:
- You have symptoms despite a "normal" TSH result
- You have unexplained fatigue, weight changes, or mood disturbance
- You have a personal or family history of autoimmune conditions
- You are planning or currently pregnant
- You are taking medications known to affect thyroid conversion (amiodarone, lithium, beta-blockers)
