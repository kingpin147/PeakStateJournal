## Reading Your Body's Nutritional Signals

Clinical deficiency — the stage where organ function is impaired and blood tests turn abnormal — is the late stage of nutritional depletion. Before that point, the body communicates suboptimal nutritional status through physical signs that are frequently misattributed to stress, aging, or genetics.

Learning to recognize these early signals allows proactive intervention before functional impairment develops.

## 1. Iron Deficiency

**Physical signs:**
- Pallor of the inner lower eyelid (pull down gently — the inside should be dark pink to red; pale suggests iron depletion)
- Spoon-shaped nails (koilonychia) in chronic deficiency
- Brittle nails with vertical ridging
- Smooth, sore tongue (glossitis)
- Cracks at the corners of the mouth (angular cheilitis)
- Restless legs syndrome — compelling urge to move legs at night

**Tests to order:** Ferritin, serum iron, TIBC, transferrin saturation, CBC with MCV and RDW

## 2. Vitamin D Deficiency

**Physical signs:**
- Generalized muscle aches and weakness (proximal muscle weakness particularly — difficulty rising from a chair, climbing stairs)
- Bone pain with pressure (press on the sternum or tibia firmly — localized tenderness suggests osteomalacia)
- Low mood and seasonal energy decline
- Frequent respiratory infections

**Tests to order:** 25-OH vitamin D (serum)

## 3. Vitamin B12 Deficiency

**Physical signs:**
- Tingling or numbness in hands and feet (symmetrical)
- Smooth, beefy red tongue
- Premature gray hair (particularly in younger adults)
- Pallor (from macrocytic anemia)
- Unsteady gait or balance problems
- Cognitive changes, brain fog, low mood

**Tests to order:** Serum B12, methylmalonic acid (MMA), homocysteine, CBC with MCV

## 4. Magnesium Deficiency

**Physical signs:**
- Eye twitching (eyelid fasciculations) — one of the most specific signs of magnesium insufficiency
- Muscle cramps, particularly nocturnal calf cramps
- Anxiety and heightened startle response
- Palpitations (irregular heartbeat awareness)
- Constipation
- Poor sleep quality

**Tests to order:** RBC magnesium (preferred over serum magnesium)

## 5. Zinc Deficiency

**Physical signs:**
- White spots on nails (leukonychia) — though this sign is not specific
- Poor wound healing
- Hair loss (diffuse)
- Loss of taste and smell (a sensitive early indicator)
- Recurring skin infections or slow-healing acne
- Reduced appetite

**Tests to order:** Serum zinc (morning, fasting); note zinc is acutely reduced by infection — test when well

## 6. Vitamin C Deficiency (Early/Subclinical)

**Physical signs:**
- Easy bruising with minor contact
- Corkscrew body hairs (perifollicular hemorrhage — pathognomonic for scurvy in advanced cases)
- Slow wound healing
- Bleeding or swollen gums around teeth
- Joint pain (particularly in the legs)
- Fatigue and low mood

**Tests to order:** Plasma vitamin C (ascorbic acid level) — though dietary history is often sufficient for diagnosis

## 7. Iodine Deficiency

**Physical signs:**
- Visible goiter (enlarged thyroid gland — a lump at the base of the throat)
- Cold intolerance
- Dry skin and hair
- Fatigue and cognitive slowing
- Constipation

**Tests to order:** Urinary iodine (spot or 24-hour collection); thyroid ultrasound if goiter present

## The Pattern Recognition Approach

Several deficiencies cluster together and are worth checking simultaneously when you identify any one of them:

- Iron + B12 + folate: Often co-deficient, particularly in those with gut inflammation or absorption issues
- Vitamin D + magnesium + K2: Fat-soluble cofactors that work synergistically
- Zinc + vitamin A: Both required for immune epithelial function; zinc required for vitamin A transport
