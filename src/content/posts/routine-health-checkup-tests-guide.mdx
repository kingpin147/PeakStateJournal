## Beyond the Standard Physical

A typical annual physical in the United States includes blood pressure measurement, a basic metabolic panel, and a standard lipid panel — a set of tests designed to catch established disease rather than detect risk trajectories. For high-functioning professionals prioritizing longevity, this bare-minimum approach leaves enormous gaps.

This guide presents a tiered testing framework: the non-negotiable annual baseline, the high-yield additions that most physicians underutilize, and the specialist-level diagnostics for a comprehensive picture.

## Tier 1: The Annual Non-Negotiable Panel

Order these tests once yearly as a minimum:

**Metabolic Health**
- Fasting blood glucose
- Fasting insulin (often requires explicit request — not automatically included)
- HbA1c
- HOMA-IR (calculated)
- Complete metabolic panel (electrolytes, kidney function, liver enzymes, glucose)

**Cardiovascular**
- Full lipid panel (total, LDL-C, HDL-C, triglycerides)
- ApoB
- hs-CRP (high-sensitivity C-reactive protein)

**Blood Count**
- CBC with differential

**Micronutrients**
- 25-OH Vitamin D
- B12 (methylcobalamin status)
- Ferritin and iron panel
- Magnesium (RBC magnesium preferred over serum)

**Thyroid**
- TSH + Free T4 + Free T3 (minimum; add TPO antibodies if any symptoms)

**Hormonal**
- Testosterone (total and free) — men over 35
- DHEA-S (adrenal reserve marker)
- Cortisol (morning, fasting) if chronic stress or fatigue is present

## Tier 2: High-Yield Tests Every 2–3 Years

**Cardiovascular depth**
- Lp(a) — once in a lifetime is sufficient; add to baseline at any age
- CAC score (Coronary Artery Calcium CT scan) — for 40+ or anyone with cardiovascular risk factors

**Cancer screening**
- Colonoscopy from age 45 (or 40 with family history of colon cancer)
- PSA with free PSA fraction — men 45+ (individualized decision with physician)
- MCED (multi-cancer early detection liquid biopsy) — emerging but increasingly available

**Metabolic depth**
- OGTT with insulin curve — if HOMA-IR or fasting insulin is borderline
- DEXA scan (body composition) — more informative than BMI for fat and muscle distribution

## Tier 3: Specialist Tests for Comprehensive Evaluation

**Epigenetics and biological aging**
- Epigenetic clock testing (biological age relative to chronological age)
- Telomere length (paired with biological age for fuller picture)

**Advanced cardiovascular**
- Carotid intima-media thickness (CIMT) — ultrasound assessment of arterial wall thickness
- Advanced lipoprotein particle testing (NMR lipoprofile for particle size/number)

**Gut health**
- Comprehensive stool microbiome analysis — not yet standard clinical care but provides microbiome diversity data
- Zonulin (intestinal permeability marker)

## Interpreting Trends Over Time

Single data points are less valuable than trajectories. Annual testing allows you to monitor:
- Is your HbA1c trending up year over year? (Act before it crosses pre-diabetes threshold)
- Is your ApoB responding to dietary changes?
- Is ferritin recovering after supplementation?

Build a personal health spreadsheet. Trends over 3–5 years are clinically more meaningful than any single result.
